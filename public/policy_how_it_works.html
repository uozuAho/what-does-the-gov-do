When you click on a policy in the MP votes page, you are presented with a chart. This chart shows each party's support of the policy, based on the way that members of those parties have voted in parliament.

The tables below the chart show the same information.

Agreement

How much each member supports a policy is determined by the 'agreement' number. In short, the more times they vote for a policy (rather than against it), the higher their agreement is. A simplified calculation:

Agreement = 100 x (votes for a policy / total number of votes held for a policy)

There are more details about how this works on They Vote For You. For example, see How "voted consistently for" is worked out for Stirling Griff on the policy For creating a federal Anti-Corruption Commission.
