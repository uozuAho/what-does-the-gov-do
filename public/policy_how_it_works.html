How to use the 'Policy' page

When you click on a policy in the MP votes page, you are presented with a chart. This chart shows each party's support of the policy, based on the way that members of those parties (MPs) have voted in parliament.

The tables below the chart show the same information.

Agreement

How much each member supports a policy is determined by the 'agreement' number. In short, the more times they vote for a policy (rather than against it), the higher their agreement is. Very roughly:

Agreement ≅ 100 x (votes for a policy / total number of votes held for a policy)

There are more details about how this works on They Vote For You. For example, see How "voted consistently for" is worked out for Stirling Griff on the policy For creating a federal Anti-Corruption Commission.

Electorates

The policy page also shows the electorates of the MPs who have voted. You only have the ability to vote for candidates in your electorate, however you may notice that MPs rarely vote against their party. This is why knowing a party's stance is important!

What is my electorate?

The easiest way to find out is to type your postcode into the search box on They Vote For You's homepage. The AEC is a more authoritative source, but their search feature is worse :). Note that these searches find your electorate, which corresponds to the 'Lower House', or House of Representatives. There is also the Senate. You vote for senators to represent the state or territory you live in. For more details on how voting works, see How Australian federal elections work.
